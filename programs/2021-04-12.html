Squat Snatch

2×2 @ 60%

1×2 @ 65%

1×1 @ 70%

1×1 @ 75%

1×1 @ 80%

1×1 @ 85%

3×1 @ 90%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 2-3 reps until you reach 60%. Improve explosiveness and stability. Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the bar and a solid deep receiving position. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between lifts.

—

 

Snatch Pull

3×3 @ 95% of Max Squat Snatch

 

Notes

Continue to the snatch pulls from where you left the squat snatches. Improve starting pull strength. Focus on a slow controlled pull with proper lifting angles. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

Conditioning

For time

5 rounds of

8 bar facing burpees over

6 clean jerks 61/43kg – 135/95lb

8 bar facing burpees over

4 ring muscle-ups

 

Notes

Start the first round at a fast manageable tempo and try to sustain it throughout the session. Similarly, go the bar facing burpees over at a steady tempo equal for the first and the last round. On the clean jerks ideally go unbroken with touch and go reps, alternatively go unbroken for as long as you can and then continue with fast single lifts. Go the ring muscle-ups unbroken. Push the last round hard.

Options

61/43kg – 135/95lb → 52/37kg – 115/80lb

Ring muscle-ups → chest to bar pull-ups

Engine

4 sets of

AMRAP 5 Minutes of

Assault bike, 30/23 calories

150 double-unders

Row, Max calories

Rest 2 minutes between

 

Notes

Try to achieve high performance in each set. Move fast and explosively through each couplet, work on the bike at 68/60+ RPM and go the double-unders unbroken for as long as possible (if you break continue immediately with the remaining reps). Then go straight to the rower and maintain a fast yet sustainable pace, the more the remaining time the steadier your pace, the less the remaining time the faster your pace. Make sure you perform fast transitions between exercises.
