Squat Clean + Front Squat + Jerk

1×2 @ 55% of Max Squat Clean

2×2 @ 60%

1×1 @ 65%

1×1 @ 70%

1×1 @ 75%

3×1 @ 80%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 2-3 reps until you reach 55%. Each rep is 1 Squat Clean + 1 Front Squat + 1 Jerk. Improve technique and consistency. Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the barbell and solid, technical jerks (work on split jerk technique). Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between reps.

Conditioning

AMRAP 21 Minutes of

20 handstand push-ups

20 calories, Row

20 wall-ball shots 9/6kg – 20/14lb

20 single arm dumbbell snatches 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb

 

Notes

The goal is to move constantly and efficiently through the 21 minutes. Maintain a fast yet manageable pace for the biggest part of the workout, 15+ minutes, then, if possible increase your pace. Ideally go the handstand push-ups and the wall-ball shots unbroken, alternatively break into a big and a smaller set with short to minimum rest between that will allow you to move efficiently. Go the single-arm dumbbell snatches at a fast steady pace. The reps are alternating, prefer to switch arms mid-air for speed. On the rower try to maintain a steady pace throughout the workout without extreme variations, 1350+ for men/1050+ for women. The key is to keep equal transitions between exercises and throughout the workout, the more you are able to keep them fast and equal the better your performance. As you reach the finish try to go unbroken for as long as possible, shorten your transitions and go for the strong finish.

Options

Handstand push-ups → push-ups hand release

22,5/15kg – 50/35lb → 15/10kg – 35/25lb

B. For form

60 GHD sit-ups

Notes

Focus on touch & go technique and efficiency. Go the GHD sit-ups unbroken for as long as proper form allows, a good way to go is with a big set and no more than 2-3 smaller sets with short rest between On the lighter snatches break into big yet manageable touch & go sets, if you realise that your touch & go form is starting to fail switch to single lifts with minimum rest between. On the heavier snatches if you are not comfortable with the load don’t hesitate to switch early or even start with solid single lifts.
