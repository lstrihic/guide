Hip Power Snatch

2×2 @ 60% of Power Snatch

1×2 @ 70%

1×1 @ 75%

2×1 @ 80%

3×1 @ 85%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and increase weight progressively with sets of 2-3 reps until you reach 60%. Improve explosiveness. The starting position of the barbell is at hip crease hight. Focus on an explosive shoulder shrug and a solid overhead position. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

—

 

Low Hang Snatch High Pull

2×3 @ 70% of Power Snatch

1×2 @ 80%

1×2 @ 90%

3×2 @ 95%

 

Notes

Continue shortly after the hip power snatches. Improve pulling power. The starting position is with the barbell just below the knees. Focus on an explosive pull and a technical finish (elbows as high as possible, barbell near the body). Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 5 seconds between lifts.

Conditioning

A. For time

30.20.10 reps of

Front squats 61/43kg – 135/95lb

Chest to bar pull-ups

 

Rest 3 minutes

 

B. For time

15.10.5 reps of

Front squats 82/52kg – 180/115lb

Bar muscle-ups

 

Notes

Try to achieve high performance in both parts. Ideally go both exercises unbroken for as long as possible breaking 2-3 reps before reaching failure, take short to minimum rest between and continue. Alternatively break from the start into manageable sets that will allow you to keep moving efficiently. Go smoothly on the first round and push harder as reps decrease. If you need to break, prefer to break the squats rather than the gymnastics.

Options

A. 61/43kg – 135/95lb → 52/37kg – 115/80lb

Chest to bar pull-ups → pull-ups

Choose pull-ups if you can’t go 10 unbroken reps of chest to bar pull-ups while fresh.

B. 82/52kg – 180/115lb → 61/43kg – 135/95lb

Bar muscle-ups → chest to bar pull-ups

Choose chest to bar pull-ups if you can’t go 5 unbroken reps of bar muscle-ups while fresh.

Gymnastics

For form

5 rounds of

6 strict weighted pull-ups 15/10kg – 35/25lb

12 strict ring dips

6 strict toes to bar

8 strict deficit handstand push-ups 10/5cm – 4/2”

 

Notes

Focus on proper form and full range of motion. Ideally go each exercise unbroken, take short yet adequate rest between exercises to achieve that. Alternatively break into a big and a smaller set with short rest between. Use a dumbbell between the legs for the pull-ups and plates on the ground for the strict deficit handstand push-ups.

Options

Strict weigted pull-ups → strict bodyweight pull-ups

Strict ring dips → ring push-ups

Strict to bar → strict knees to chest

Strict deficit handstand push-ups → regular strict handstand push-ups

Notes

Try to achieve maximum performance in both parts. Push fast and efficiently through the couplet, move explosively on the rower 1400+ for men — 1150+ for women and immediately continue to the burpees/double-unders. On the max rep set maintain a fast, sustainable pace, the more the remaining time the steadier your pace, the less the remaining time the faster your pace.
