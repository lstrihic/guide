Conditioning

For time

100 pull-ups

80 wall-balls 9/6kg – 20/14lb.

60 calories, Row

40 single-leg squats

20 bar muscle-ups

Cap: 25 minutes

 

Notes

Warm up

Row, 800 meters @ easy pace

Then,

2 rounds of

8 pull-ups

16 wall-bals

6 bar muscle-ups

12 single-leg squats

Goal

Be consistent throughout the session, each movement has it’s own difficulty and needs different handling, perform smooth transitions and focus on the movements separately like each movement is a different workout.

Movements

Break the pull-ups into manageable sets. Ideally start with a big set and no more than 3-4 smaller sets after that. Go the wall-ball unbroken for as long as you can, take minimum rest and continue with the remaining reps, alternatively break into big manageable sets. Row at a fast pace. +1300 for men/+1100 for women, and move steadily and unbroken on the single-leg squats. If you are comfortable with gymnastics attempt to go unbroken for as long as you can, if not break from the start into a big, and no more than 2-3 smaller sets.

Options

Bar muscle-ups —> chest to bar pull-ups

Engine – Rowing

A. 3 sets of

Row, 1500 meters @ 22 strokes/minute

Rest 2 minutes between sets

Pace: 2km PR pace + 16 seconds

Rest 5 minutes

B. 2 sets of

Row, 600 meters @ free rate

Rest 1 minute between sets

Pace: 2km PR pace + 7 seconds

 

Notes

Warm up & Cool down

Row 800 meters at an easy pace before and after the main session

Goal

The session consists of 2 portions, in each portion you have to maintain a certain pace and stroke rate and be consistent throughout the given sets. Make sure you primarily keep steady strokes and then achieve the prescribed time/distance pace. In the second portion strokes are free, find the optimal stroke rate for you considering that more strokes mean higher heart rate and less strokes mean more powerful pulls. Start with fewer strokes and increase.
