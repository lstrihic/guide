Hang Power Snatch

2×2 @ 70%

2×2 @ 75%

2×1 @ 80%

4×1 @ 85%

 

Warm up

3 sets of

2 snatch high pulls + 3 hang power snatches + 4 half squat snatch balance @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 hang power snatches until 70%.

Goal

Work on explosiveness and confidence on the hang position. Rest no less than 75 seconds between sets.

Movements

First pull the barbell to hip height, then focus on an explosive pull and a solid receiving position. Make sure you drive smoothly and under control the barbell back to the hip crease and avoid spending too much time under tension holding the barbell at the hang position. In each set reposition swiftly and continue to the second rep.

Conditioning

For time

5 rounds of

Row, 350 meters

12 double dumbbell snatches 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb

100 double-unders

15 meters, Double dumbbell shoulders rack lunges 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb

Cap: 25 minutes

 

Warm up

1000 meters, Row @ easy pace

Then,

3.6.9 reps of

Down-ups

American kettlebell swings 24/16kg – 53/35lb.

Kettlebell goblet lunges

Goal & Movements

Row at a fast yet sustainable pace throughout the session that will allow you to breathe and move well on the exercises that follow the row. Ideally, go the double dumbbell snatches unbroken at a steady tempo, alternatively break into a big and a smaller set with short to minimum rest between. Go the double-unders unbroken for as long as possible and break the shoulders rack lunges distance into 2×7,5-meter unbroken distances. In the end, taken that the exercises are easily manageable, it comes down to how fast and efficiently you will push through the lunges.

Options

22,5/15kg – 50/35lb —> 15/10kg – 50/35lb.

Barbell Cycling

EMOM 10 Minutes of

4 power cleans

3 thrusters

2 front squats

1 jerk

 

Warm up

Sets of 2 power cleans + 2 thrusters + 1 front squat + 1 jerk until you reach 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

Goal & Movements

Start from 61/43kg – 135/95lb and progressively add weight on the barbell until you reach the heaviest load with which you can complete the set unbroken. Ideally, make short smart weight increases so that you challenge yourself to complete the EMOM with the heaviest possible weight.
