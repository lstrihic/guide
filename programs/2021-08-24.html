Warm up

2 sets of

3 clean pulls + 3 hang power cleans + 3 hang squat cleans + 2 presses + 2 push presses + 2 jerks @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 squat cleans + 2 jerks until 60%.

Goal

Improve explosiveness and stability. The lifts at 60% are 2 times of 2 squat cleans + 2 jerks. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between squat cleans.

Movements

Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the barbell and solid receiving positions. Work and build confidence on split jerks since this is the most appropriate jerk technique when it comes to heavy weights.

—

 

Clean Pull

3×3 @ 95% of Max Squat Clean

 

Warm up

Continue from the percentage you left the squat clean + jerk.

Goal

Improve strength. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

Movements

Focus on a controlled pull until the barbell is over the knees, then increase speed and finish with an explosive shoulder shrug.

Conditioning

AMRAP 20 Minutes of

20 pull-ups

20 wall-balls 9/6kg – 20/14lb.

20 handstand push-ups

20 sumo deadlifts high pull 32/24kg – 70/53lb.

 

Warm up

2 rounds of

6 pull-ups

12 wall-balls

6 handstand push-ups

12 sumo deadlift high pulls

Goal

The goal is to move constantly and efficiently through the 20 minutes. Maintain a fast yet manageable pace for the biggest part of the workout, 15+ minutes, then if possible increase your pace and go for the strong finish.

Movements

Ideally go each exercise unbroken with smooth transitions between them that will allow you to do so and push these transitions harder as you reach the finish. Alternatively, break the pull-ups and/or handstand push ups into a big and no more than 1-2 smaller sets depending on your comfort on each exercise and try to go the wall-balls and SDHPs unbroken.

Options

32/24kg – 70/53lb. —> 24/16kg – 53/35lb.

Warm up

2-3 sets of

9/5 calories, Assault bike

3 overhead squats 70%

Rest 60-90 seconds between

Goal & Movements

The goal is to find the fastest pace on the assault bike that will allow you to complete successfully, efficiently and without excessive rest the overhead squats. Move fast on the assault bike 70+ for men | 63+ for women RPM and take a breath before you start the overhead squats. Try to establish a solid overhead position and go the squats unbroken in each round, then go once again straight to the bike. As the weight on the barbell increases the tempo of the squats decreases but the position should remain the same.

Options

20/15 calories, Assault bike —> 16/12 calories, Echo bike
