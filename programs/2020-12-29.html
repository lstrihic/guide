Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and continue with sets of 2-3 reps until you reach 70%. Improve explosiveness and stability. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Conditioning

2 sets of

AMRAP 8 Minutes of

2.4.6.8…etc reps of

Power cleans 70/50kg – 155/110lb.

Double dumbbell thrusters 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb.

Bar muscle-ups

Rest 4 minutes between sets

 

Notes

Try to achieve maximum performance in each set. Prefer to go with single lifts and maintain a fast and super steady tempo as reps with each round increase. Instead, really focus on going the double dumbbell thrusters unbroken for as long as possible (stop ideally 1-2 reps before failure). Alternatively, once reps of each round get more than you can handle break the thrusters into a big and smaller set with short to minimum rest between. On the muscle ups go the low-rep rounds unbroken and have a plan of breaking the high-rep rounds into manageable sets (depending on your level) that will allow you to push through the total volume efficiently. Don’t expect your performance to be equal, give your best to push through each set as efficiently as possible.

Options

70/50kg – 155/110lb → 60/43kg – 135/95lb

22,5/15kg – 50/35lb → 15/10kg – 35/22,5lb

Barbell Cycling

A. For form*

30 squat snatches 60%

 

B. For form

20 squat snatches 70%

 

C. For form

10 squat snatches 80%

 

Notes

There is no need to force touch & go sets, prefer to go with single lifts at a fast, steady tempo. Focus on consistency, being able to take short equal rest between lifts is challenging. So the more you succeed in maintaining a steady tempo throughout each portion the better your performance will be. Go for a touch & go set only when you reach the finish of each portion if you feel like challenging yourself and pushing your limits.

*Rest 3 minutes between portions.

Gymnastics

For form

3 L-sit rope climbs*

6 leg-less rope climbs

9 regular rope climbs

 

Notes

Work on efficiency. Focus on proper form and technique. Take adequate rest between rope climbs. You should be able to distinct techniques among the 3 styles. On the L- sit rope climbs focus on a strict, solid L-sit position. The leg-less rope climbs need proper coordination between lower and upper body, ascent with kicks or kipping. On the regular rope climbs squat on the rope and assist your upper body as much as you can.

Descend on the L-sit rope climbs in the same way that you ascended, descend on the leg-less and regular rope climbs smoothly by sliding or extending and jumping down.

Options

3.6.9 → 2.4.6 ascents of given exercises

Run, 400 meters @ 1’23”-1’35”

Start a new set every 3 minutes

 

Notes

Run 1600 meters and perform some sprinting drills as warm up. Try to achieve high performance in each set. Select a pace within the given time frame that will allow you to be consistent throughout the session. This is a demanding sprinting distance so don’t worry if your performance is slightly dropping with each set but make sure you don’t start too hard and destroy yourself too soon. Jog for 800 meters as cool down.
