Hang Squat Clean

1×2 @ 60%

1×2 @ 70%

1×2 @ 75%

1×1 @ 80%

3×1 @ 90%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 2-3 reps until you reach 60%. Work on explosiveness and consistency at the hang position. Focus on an explosive pull and a super fast drive under the barbell.  Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Conditioning

For time

3 rounds of

12 bar-facing burpees over

9 overhead squats 52/37kg – 115/80lb.

6 power snatches 52/37kg – 115/80lb.

 

Rest 3 minutes

 

3 rounds of

12 bar-facing burpees over

9 front squats 52/37kg – 115/80lb.

6 clean and jerks 52/37kg – 115/80lb.

Cap: 15 minutes including rest time.

 

Notes

This workout consists of 2 portions, try to achieve high performance in each one. Go the bar-facing burpees over at a steady sustainable pace throughout the session. The goal is to go the overhead squats/front squats unbroken and the power snatches/clean and jerks unbroken as well using touch and go technique. If not possible for unbroken, prefer to go the 1st and 2nd round with single lifts of power snatches/clean and jerks with minimum rest between and attempt to go touch and go the last set if you feel confident enough.

Max Effort

For time

21 calories, Assault bike

Then

2 rounds of

12 single arm dumbbell hang squat snatch 30/22,5kg – 70/50lb.

6 ring muscle-ups

Cap: 4 minutes

 

Notes

Start strong and go as fast as you can, perform fast transitions and push yourself to your limits. Start with 21 calories on the bike, be explosive and push fast through the cals then go straight to the hang squat snatches. Ideally, the ring muscle-ups should be performed unbroken and even at a fast tempo. On the snatches try to maintain a fast sold tempo that will allow you to keep moving efficiently, reps are alternating.

Options

30/22,5kg – 70/50lb.  —> 22,5/15kg / 50/35lb.

Engine – Running

A. 2000 meters @ easy pace

 

B. 1000 meters @ 4’30”-5’20”/km

1000 meters @ 4’20”-5’10”/km

1000 meters @ 4’10”-5’00”/km

1000 meters @ 4’00”-4’50”/m

1000 meters @ Max effort

 

Notes

Warm up by running 2000 meters prior the main session and cool down by running 400 meters afterwards at a comfortable pace of your choice. The combination of the prescribed paces and the 6000 meter distance is demanding, so select manageable paces within the given time frames in order to progressively increase and successfully reach the intended max effort pace finish.
