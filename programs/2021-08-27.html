Power Snatch

1×2 @ 60%

1×2 @ 70%

1×2 @ 80%

2×1 @ 85%

3×1 @ 90%

 

Warm up

3 sets of

2 snatch high pulls + 3 power snatches @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2-3 power snatches until 60%.

Goal

Improve strength and stability. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between lifts.

Movements

Focus on an explosive pull maintaining the barbell close to your body and a solid overhead receiving position.

—

 

Snatch High Pull

2×2 @ 95% of Max Power Snatch

2×2 @ 100%

 

Warm up

Continue from the percentage you left the power snatch.

Goal

Improve technique and weightlifting angles. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

Movements

Focus on a controlled pull until the barbell is over the knees, then increase speed and extend your body until the barbell is as high as possible. Keep the barbell close to your body’s vertical line.

Conditioning

For time

Bike-erg, 1500 meters

75 power snatches 35/25kg – 75/55lb.

Cap: 10/11 minutes

 

Warm up

250 meters, Bike-erg @ moderate pace

10 touch & go power snatches

250 meters, Bike-erg @ moderate pace

5 touch & go power snatches

Goal & Movements

Go the bike fast but at a sustainable pace that will allow you to breath well, then push through the snatches with a big unbroken set with almost as many touch and go reps as you can and continue putting effort with smaller unbroken sets of touch and go reps until you complete the total reps.

Options

Engine

8 sets of

Row, 1000 meters @ 26 strokes/minute

Rest 2 minutes between sets

Pace : 2km PR pace + 7 seconds

 

Warm up

4 minutes @ easy pace using the warm up routines (1st minute only arm pulls, 2nd only leg pulls, 3rd and 4th minute regular rowing)

Rest 1 minute

300 meters @ 2km PR pace + 2 seconds

Goal

Maintain high performance in each set efficiently keeping the given pace (2km PR pace + 7 seconds) and most importantly steady 26 strokes per minute. The given stroke rate is a moderate tempo ideal for focus on technique.

Cool down

4 minutes @ very light pressure.
