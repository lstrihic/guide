Squat Snatch

1×3 @ 60%

1×2 @ 70%

1×2 @ 75%

1×2 @ 80%

1×1 @ 85%

1×1 @ 90%

2×1 @ 75%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and increase weight progressively with sets of 2-3 reps until you reach 60%. Improve explosiveness and technique. Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the bar and a solid deep receiving position. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

Conditioning

For time

100 single arm dumbbell snatches 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb

80 box-jumps 60/50cm – 24/20”

60 single arm dumbbell hang clean and press 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb

40 burpee box-jumps 60/50cm – 24/20”

 

Notes

The descending reps give you the psychological advantage so make sure you keep high effort throughout. The single arm dumbbell snatches and single arm dumbbell hang clean and presses are alternating, switch arms with each rep preferably mid-air for extra speed. On the box-jumps (ideally go touch & go) and burpee box-jumps maintain a fast, steady pace. In general this workout needs consistency, the weights are not that heavy and keeping the engine going is all that you need.

Options

22,5/15kg – 50/35lb → 15/10kg – 35/25lb

Gymnastics

EMOM 15 Minutes of

1st: 3-5 ring muscle-ups

2nd: 10-12 strict deficit handstand push-ups 10/5cm – 4/2”

3rd: 16-20 toes to bar

 

Notes

The goal is to move efficiently and with quality through the 15 minutes. Depending on your level and condition choose a number of reps from the prescribed for each exercise that you will be able to complete in each minute and consistently through the workout.

Options

Ring muscle-ups → chest to rings pull-ups

Deficit → regular strict handstand push-ups

Toes to bar → knees to chest
