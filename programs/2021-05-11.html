Power Clean & Jerk

2×2 @ 75%

1×2 @ 80%

1×1 @ 85%

3×1 @ 90%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 3 reps until you reach 75%. Improve power and stability. Focus on a powerful pull and an explosive solid jerk. Prefer to go with split jerks and work on technique. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between lifts.

20 deadlifts 124/83kg – 275/185lb.

40 toes to bar

60 wall-balls 9/6kg – 20/14lb.

Cap: 20 minutes

 

Notes

In this workout you find two sequences of the same exercises and you need to follow two tempos. Go at a steadier and more manageable pace until you complete the 40 calories on the row and then increase effort and try to reach a faster pace that will lead to a strong finish. If you are really comfortable with wall-ball shots go the first set unbroken, if not break into a big and no more than 1-2 smaller sets with minimum rest between. Prefer to break the toes to bars and deadlifts in the first sequence into manageable sets that will allow you to move efficiently. In the inverse second sequence of the exercises go each one unbroken for as long as possible. Row at  at 1350+ for men | 1000+ for women and increase as you push through the cals.

Barbell Cycling – Power Snatch

10 reps @ 60%

8 reps @ 70%

6 reps @ 80%

4 reps @ 85%

2 reps @ 90%

 

Notes

Consider this session as a For time but focus on practicing barbell efficiency. Go the lightweight percentages with a touch and go set and then switch to single lifts. Continue to the heavier percentages with solid single lifts at a fast tempo (minimum rest between lifts). The better you manage keeping the resting time between lightweight lifts and between heavyweight lifts equal the better your performance will be.

Engine – Running

A. 20 minutes @ easy pace

 

B. Every 2 Minutes for 20 Minutes (10 sets)

200 meters @ 37”-55”

 

Notes

Warm up for 15 minutes prior to the main session and cool down for 10 minutes afterwards running at a comfortable pace of your choice. Go for a finish ideally within 45 seconds in each set, working up to 95-97% of Vo2max. Walk to recover and regulate your breathing with take big nasal breaths while resting.
