Hang Squat Snatch

2×2 @ 65% of Max Hang Squat Snatch

2×2 @ 70%

2×1 @ 75%

2×1 @ 80%

2×1 @ 85%

2×1 @ 90%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight until you reach 65%, go with sets of 2 reps. Work on the hang position and technique. Focus on a fast drive under the barbell, solid deep squat and overhead positions. Try to spend minimum time at the hang position, adding the less possible pressure to your back and grips. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

—

 

Snatch Pull from blocks

1×3 @ 95% of Max Hang Squat Snatch

1×3 @ 100%

2×3 @ 105%

 

Notes

Continue to the snatch pulls from where you left the hang squat snatches. Focus on an powerful pull with an explosive shoulder shrug as a finish. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

75 wall-balls 9/6kg – 20/14lb

50 double dumbbell power cleans 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb

Cap: 18 minutes

 

Notes

Move steadily through the total volume with smooth transitions between exercises. Maintain a steady pace at 1300+ for men / 980+ for women and continue by breaking each exercise into a big and as few smaller sets as possible with short rest between. Focus on movement mechanics on the dumbbell exercises and proper butterfly technique on the chest to bar pull-ups.

Options

Chest to bar pull-ups → pull-ups

*Go with pull-ups if you can’t perform at least 10 unbroken chest to bar pull-ups

Strokes:

3000 meters @ 22/min

1500 meters @ 24/min

Notes

Row 4 minutes at an easy pace and then spend another 2 minutes at the starting pace of the main session, 2km PR pace + 16 seconds. In the main session the goal is to work efficiently in each portion and finish with a max effort 375-meter distance. In each portion, maintain the given pace and steady strokes, continue rowing at super light pressure during the resting period between sets.
