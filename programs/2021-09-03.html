Squat Snatch

1×3 @ 60%

2×2 @ 65%

2×2 @ 70%

1×2 @ 80%

1×1 @ 85%

3×2 @ 90%

 

Warm up

2 sets of

3 snatch high pulls + 3 hang power snatches + 3 overhead squats + 3 squat snatches @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2-3 squat snatches until 60%.

Goal

Improve explosiveness and stability. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Movements

Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the barbell and a solid deep receiving position.

—

 

Snatch Pull

2×2 @ 95% of Max Squat Snatch

2×2 @ 100%

2×2 @ 105%

 

Warm up

Continue from the percentage that you left the squat snatch.

Goal

Work on strength. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

Movements

Focus on a slow pull with proper angles and finish with an explosive shoulder shrug. Barbell should remain near the body at all times.

Strength/Gymnastics

For form

3 rounds of

30 meters, Handstand walk

30 single-leg squats

10 double kettlebell snatches 24/16kg – 53/35lb.

 

Warm up

20 shoulder taps

5 double kettlebell swings

10 narrow squats

5 double kettlebell snatches

10 meters, Handstand walk

Goal

Focus on proper form and full range of motion. Try to maintain a steady tempo throughout the 3 rounds.

Movements

Go the handstand walk by 10-meter unbroken distances. The single-leg squats are alternating, go at a fast yet steady tempo with focus on proper reps concerning depth of squat and lockout. On the double-unders ideally go unbroken, alternatively break into a big and smaller set with short to minimum rest between.

Options

Handstand walk —> 5 wall-walks

Single-leg squats —> goblet narrow squats with dumbbell

24/16kg – 53/35lb —> 20/12kg – 44/26lb.

Engine

Row, 5000 meters

Rate 22 strokes/minute

Pace 2km PR pace + 12 seconds

 

Warm up

4 minutes @ easy pace warm up routines (1st minute only arm pulls, 2nd only leg pulls, 3rd and 4th minute regular rowing)

Rest 1 minute

2 sets of

2 minutes @ 2km PR pace + 10 seconds.

Goal

The goal is to row the given distance smoothly with proper form and technique maintaining exactly the given stroke rate and pace with priority on the stroke rate. Go with slow, full and powerful pulls.
