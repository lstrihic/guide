Power Clean & Jerk

2×2 @ 75%

1×2 @ 80%

1×1 @ 85%

3×1 @ 90%

 

Warm up

2 sets of

3 clean pulls + 3 hang power cleans + 2 presses + 2 push presses + 2 jerks @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 power cleans & jerk until 75%.

Goal

Improve power and stability. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Movements

Focus on a powerful pull, a solid front rack receiving position and an explosive jerk. Prefer to go with split jerks if you wish to improve your technique since the power clean percentages are ideal for that. Go with push jerks if you need to focus more on the explosiveness of the push.

—

 

Clean Pull

1×3 @ 90% of Max Power Clean

1×3 @ 100%

2×3 @ 105%

 

Warm up

Continue from the percentage that you left the power clean & jerk.

Goal

Improve strength. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

Movements

Focus on a controlled pull until the barbell is over the knees, then increase speed and finish with an explosive shoulder shrug.

15 pull-ups

Rest 1 minute

Then,

2 rounds of

15 deadlifts 124/83kg – 245/185lb.

15 chest to bar pull-ups

Rest 1 minute

Then,

15 deadlifts 143/90kg – 315/205lb.

15 bar muscle-ups

Cap: 15 minutes (including rest)

 

Warm up

Shoulder mobility

Then,

3 rounds of

12 double kettlebell deadlifts < 24kg – 53lb.

10 ring rows

8 swings on the pull-up bar

Goal

In each next portion the volume of the session decreases yet the difficulty increases. As the weight of the deadlifts increases and the gymnastics are becoming more demanding break into sets accordingly and try to maintain your effort equal.

Movements

Break the deadlifts into a big and no more than 2-3 smaller sets with short to minimum rest between. Ideally go each round of gymnastics unbroken, alternatively break into a big and a smaller set. As the barbell gets really heavy don’t hesitate to go with solid single lifts at a steady tempo.

Options

83/61kg – 185/135lb.

102/70kg – 225/155lb

143/90kg – 315/205lb.

Bar muscle-ups —> chest to bar pull-ups

Goal & Movements

The goal is to walk fast and efficiently on your hands under fatigue. Go the double-unders unbroken for as long as you can, if you break immediately continue with the remaining reps. Break the handstand walk into 10-meter unbroken distances. Make sure you take equal, and ideally minimum, rest between handstand walks.

Then

Max calories, Echo or Assault bike

 

Rest 5 minutes

 

B. AMRAP 10 Minutes of

Buy in:

Ski-erg, 2km

Then

Max calories, Echo or Assault bike

 

Rest 5 minutes

 

C. AMRAP 5 Minutes of

Buy in:

Ski-erg, 1km

Then

Max calories, Echo or Assault bike

 

Goal & Movements

Try to achieve maximum performance in each portion. It’s all about pace, maintain a steady sustainable pace on the ski-erg in each portion and base the intensity of the echo or assault bike on the remaining time  after the ski-erg. In each portion the work volume decreases allowing the body to work inside different energy zones. Go part A at 70-75% of VO2MAX with focus on the aerobic threshold (70% aerobic – 30% anaerobic threshold), part B  at 80-85% of VO2MAX with focus on both aerobic and anaerobic thresholds (50% aerobic – 50% anaerobic threshold) and part C at 90-95% of VO2MAX with focus on the anaerobic threshold (30% aerobic – 70% anaerobic threshold).
