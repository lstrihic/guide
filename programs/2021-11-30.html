Power Clean with 2” pause below knees

2×2 @ 55% of Max Power Clean

1×2 @ 60%

1×1 @ 65%

1×1 @ 70%

3×1 @ 75%

 

Warm up

3 sets of

3 clean pulls until below knees + 3 hang power cleans below knees @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 power cleans with 2” pause below knees until 55%.

Goal

Work on explosiveness and confidence on the pull pause position. The position of the barbell is 10cm below the knees. Rest no less than 75 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between lifts.

Movements

First deadlift the barbell to a height 10cm below your knees, pause for 2 seconds at a solid position and continue with an explosive pull.

Squat Clean + Front Squat + Jerk

1×2+1+1 @ 55% of Max Squat Clean

2×2+1+1 @ 60%

1×2+1+1 @ 65%

1×2+1+1 @ 70%

1×2+1+1 @ 75%

3×2+1+1 @ 80%

 

Warm up

2 sets of

3 clean pulls + 3 hang power cleans + 3 hang squat cleans + 2 presses + 2 push presses + 2 jerks @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 squat cleans + 1 front squats + 1 jerk until 55%.

Goal

Improve explosiveness and consistency. The lifts at 60% are 2 times of 2 squat cleans + 1 front squat + 1 jerk. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between squat cleans.

Movements

Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the barbell and solid receiving positions. Maintain proper form on the squats and work on powerful push jerks.

Goal & Movements

Maintain a steady pace on the bike throughout the session that will allow you to breathe and perform well on the exercises. If you are comfortable with bar muscle ups try to go unbroken, if not, prefer to break the first two rounds into a big and a smaller set. Overhead squats are the challenge, ideally go unbroken at a steady tempo, alternatively, break into a big and a smaller set with short rest between. Remember that every drop costs time.

Barbell Cycling

10 power cleans @ 70% of Max Power Clean

8 power cleans @ 75%

6 power cleans @ 80%

4 power cleans @ 85%

2 power cleans @ 90%

 

Warm up

Sets of 3-5 power cleans until 70% of max power clean.

Goal

The goal is to move fast and efficiently with the barbell and the challenge is to maintain equal or similar rest between lifts as the load on the barbell increases.

Movements

Go the lightweight lifts unbroken using touch and go technique, as weight gets heavier switch to solid single lifts.
