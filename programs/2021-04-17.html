Hang Squat Clean

2×3 @ 65%

2×2 @ 70%

1×2 @ 75%

1×2 @ 80%

4×1 @ 85%

 

Notes

Same as on Friday, today the work is on Clean. Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight until you reach 65%, go with sets of 2 reps. Work on the hang position and technique. Focus on a fast drive under the barbell, a solid deep squat and front rack position. Try to spend minimum time at the hang position, adding the less possible pressure to your back and grips. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Conditioning

For time

15 squat snatches 61/43kg – 135/95lb

13 ring muscle-ups

11 squat snatches 70/48kg – 155/105lb

9 ring muscle-ups

7 squat snatches 82/52kg – 180/115lb

5 ring muscle-ups

Cap: 11 minutes

 

Notes

Work on the commonly used combination of squat snatches and ring muscle-ups. Go the squat snatches with single lifts, try to maintain a steady pace with minimum rest between lifts. The better you manage equal rest between light lifts and heavy lifts the better your performance will be. Ideally go the ring muscle-ups unbroken, alternatively break into a big and a smaller sets with short to minimum rest between. Increase weight on the barbell on your own.

Max Effort

For time

20 double dumbbell power cleans 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb

20 meters, Handstand walk

20 calories, Assault bike

Sprint 20 meters to the finish line

Cap: 4 minutes

 

Notes

Go each exercise unbroken for as long as possible. Break the handstand walk into two 10-meter unbroken distances and be super explosive on the bike. Mark a 20 meter distance away from the bike as a finish line. Transitions will play an important role, make sure you get on and off the bike fast and efficiently.
