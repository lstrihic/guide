Conditioning

For time

2 rounds of

Row, 60/45 calories

40 single-arm dumbbell snatches 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb

20 bar muscle-ups

Cap: 18 minutes

 

Notes

Pace the 1st round, push the 2nd round harder by going for the unbroken and shortening your transitions between exercises. Row at a steady pace in both rounds, 1300+ for men /1000+ for women, go the single-arm dumbbell snatches at a steady tempo with focus on technical, smooth repetitions. In the first rounds break the muscle-ups into big, manageable sets with short rest between, in the second round go unbroken for as long as possible (break 1-2 reps before failure).

Options

22,5/15kg – 50/35lb → 15/10kg – 35/25lb

20 → 15 → 10 reps of

Bar muscle-ups → chest to bar pull-ups

*Go with chest to bar pull-ups if you can’t perform at least 5 unbroken reps of bar muscle-ups while fresh

Engine

A. Row 1000m

*using the warm up routines. 

 

B. Main session

 

B1. Row 2500 meters @ 20 strokes/minute

Pace: 2km PR pace + 16 seconds

 

Rest 3 minutes

 

B2. Row 2500 meters @ 22 strokes/minute

Pace: 2km PR pace + 14 seconds

 

Rest 3 minutes

 

B3. Row 2500 meters @ 20 strokes/minute

Pace: 2km PR pace + 12 seconds

 

Notes

Row 1000 meters at low pressure as warm-up (*go the 1st 250 meters only using legs, the next 250m only using arms and then last 500 meters executing the whole movement). In the main session try to achieve high performance in each portion. Focus on primarily maintaining exactly the strokes prescribed and secondarily achieving the given time/meters pace. Working on strokes will help you efficiently control your pace and your cardio vs strength balance. Row 5 minutes at very low pressure as cool-down.
