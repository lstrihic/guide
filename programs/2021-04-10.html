Power Snatch + Hang Power Snatch

Daily Max

 

Notes

Reach a heavy lift for the day with sets of 2 power snatches + 2 hang power snatches < 60% and continue with as few sets as possible of 1 power snatch + 1 hang power snatch. Improve power and stability. Focus on powerful pulls and a solid hang position. The less time you spend on the hang the better it is for your back and grips. Rest no less than 90 seconds between set.

—

 

Power Clean + Hang Power Clean + Jerk

Daily Max

 

Notes

Reach a heavy lift for the day with sets of 1 power clean + 1 hang power clean,  1 hang power clean + 2 jerks < 60% and continue with as few sets as possible of 1 power clean + 1 hang power clean + 1 jerk. Same as the previous session with the snatches. Improve power and stability. Focus on powerful pulls and a solid hang position. Perform the hang power clean fast, but don’t rush to perform the jerk, give yourself some time to find a solid front rack position and focus. Rest no less than 90 seconds between set.

Conditioning

For time

50 overhead squats 43/30kg – 95/65lb

30 bar-facing burpees over

10 ring muscle-ups

50 single arm dumbbell snatches 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb

30 bar-facing burpees over

10 ring muscle-ups

 

Notes

Go this workout at two tempos, pace until the first ring muscle-ups and then start pushing harder. Go the overhead squats unbroken for as long as possible, break 1-2 reps before you feel like reaching muscle failure and then continue with a few smaller sets, maintain a steady tempo on the bar-facing burpees over that will allow you to breathe and break the ring muscle-ups into a big and a smaller set with short to minimum rest between. The single arm dumbbell snatches are alternating, go at a fast, steady tempo and try to perform technical mid-air transitions. On the second round of burpees move at an equal or faster pace than in the first round, then catch a breath and go the last ring muscle ups unbroken for as long as possible.

Options

Ring muscle-ups → chest to bar pull-ups

Max Effort

For time

15 ring muscle-ups

10 sandbag clean over shoulders 70/50kg – 155/110lb

5 ring muscle-ups

Sprint 20 meters to the finish line

Cap: 4 minutes

 

Notes

This is a competition based workout. Test your limits and push hard from the start. If you are comfortable with ring muscle-ups attempt to go the 15 reps unbroken far as long as possible, break just before failure and continue with the remaining reps, if not, prefer to break from the start into 2-3 manageable sets that will allow you to push efficiently through the reps. Go straight to the sandbag clean over shoulders and maintain a fast steady tempo, then catch a breath and go for an unbroken set of muscle-ups. Keep in mind that in any case you should keep your rest to the minimum possible. Ideally mark a 20-meter distance from the muscle-up spot to an imagery finish line.

Options

Ring muscle-ups → chest to bar pull-ups
