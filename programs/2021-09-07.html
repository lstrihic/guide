Warm up

2 sets of

3 clean pulls + 3 hang power cleans @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 deadlifts until 10cm below the knees + 1 power clean until 55%.

Goal

Improve explosiveness. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Movements

First deadlift the barbell until 10cm below the knees, hold for 2 seconds remaining still, and then continue the movement until the barbell is on the front rack. Focus on a solid hold position below the knees and an explosive pull. Hip crease should be over 90 degrees.

1×2+2+1 @ 65%

1×1+2+1 @ 70%

1×1+2+1 @ 75%

3×1+2+1 @ 80%

 

Warm up

2 sets of

3 clean pulls + 3 hang power cleans + 3 hang squat cleans + 2 presses + 2 push presses + 2 jerks @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 squat cleans + 2 front squats + 1 jerk until 60%.

Goal

Improve explosiveness and consistency. The lifts at 60% are 2 times of 2 squat cleans + 2 front squats + 1 jerk. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between squat cleans.

Movements

Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the barbell and solid receiving positions. Maintain proper form on the squats and work on powerful push jerks.

B. For form

14 calories, Assault bike

5.4.3.2.1 reps of

Shoulders to overhead 83/57kg – 185/125lb.

 

Warm up

Build up to 70/52kg – 155/115lb. of shoulder to overhead

Then,

5 calories, Assault bike

3.2.1 reps of

Shoulder to overhead 70/52kg – 155/115lb.

Goal & Movements

The goal is to move efficiently with the barbell under metabolic fatigue created by the assault bike. Try to achieve maximum performance in each set. Push fast and explosively through the assault bike, 73+ for men / 65+ RPM for women then go straight to the shoulder to overhead. Ideally go the shoulder to overhead unbroken at a steady tempo with focus on smooth and efficient rebound on the shoulders. Alternatively, break the high-rep rounds into no more than 2-3 manageable sets.

Options

A. 70/52kg – 155/115lb. —> 61/43kg – 135/95lb

B. 83/57kg – 185/125lb. —> 70/52kg – 155/115lb
