Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 3 reps until you reach 55%. Each rep is 1 Squat Snatch + 1 Overhead Squat. Improve technique and explosiveness. Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the barbell and solid overhead squats. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between reps.

Snatch Pull

5×3 @ 85% of Max Squat Snatch

 

Notes

Continue to the snatch pulls from where you left the squat snatches + overhead squats. Work on pulling strength and technique (proper angles on the second pull). Focus on a slow, strong starting pull, once the barbel is over the knees increase your speed and finish with an explosive second pull to full range of motion. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 2 minutes between sets, no more than 5 seconds between lifts.

B. For time

27.21.15.9 reps of

Toes to bar

Thrusters 43/30kg – 95/65lb

Cap: 8 minutes

 

Notes

Try to achieve maximum performance in both parts. The descending reps give you the psychological advantage, the 2nd part is considerably lighter than the first one so your performance should be faster or at least equal. In the 1st part break the hig-rep rounds (27, 21 reps) of both exercises into no more than 2-3 sets with minimum rest between and try to go the low-rep rounds unbroken. In the 3nd part go unbroken for as long as possible, if you need to break an exercise prefer to break the toes to bar and put effort on moving efficiently and unbroken on the thrusters.

Notes

Focus on proper form and technique. Push fast and explosively through the ski and complete the calories quickly. Then take a breath and go for an unbroken set of muscle ups. Take short yet adequate rest between rounds to achieve that. The more you utilise your legs on the ski and the more technical you are on the ring muscle ups, the more efficiently you will move through the total volume.
