Squat Snatch

2×3 @ 60%

2×2 @ 65%

1×2 @ 70%

1×2 @ 75%

3×2 @ 80%

1×5 @ 70%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and increase weight progressively with sets of 2-3 reps until you reach 60%. Work on technique and explosiveness. Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the barbell and a solid bottom position. The lightweight sets allow you to really focus on technique and quality. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

—

 

Squat Clean & Jerk

2×2+2 @ 50%

2×2+1 @ 60%

1×2+1 @ 65%

1×2+1 @ 70%

3×3+3 @ 75%

1×5+1 @ 65%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 2 squat cleans + 2 jerks until you reach 50%. The lifts at 50% are 2 sets of 2 squat cleans + 2 jerks, at 75% are 3 sets of 3 squat cleans + 3 jerks. Go the squat cleans with single lifts. Focus on a technical and fast clean and explosive jerks. The percentages allow you to really work on explosiveness and technique. Rest no less than 2 minutes between sets.

Conditioning

For time

4 rounds of

15 power snatches 35/25kg – 75/55lb

12 bar-facing burpees over

9 thrusters 35/25kg – 75/55lb

Cap: 8 minutes

 

Notes

Ideally go the power snatches unbroken using touch and go technique, alternatively break from the start into a big and a smaller set with short rest between. On the burpees maintain a fast, sustainable pace throughout the workout. The thrusters should be performed unbroken, if you have to choose, prefer to break the power cleans and go the thrusters unbroken. In any case push the last round harder by increasing your pace and shortening your transitions. Consider that the barbell is lightweight and if you have the capacity to go unbroken, transitions will play the biggest role to your performance.

1×5 @ 65%

1×4 @ 70%

1×3 @ 75%

1×2 @ 80%

5×1 @ 85%

 

Notes

Build up with sets of 4-5 reps until you reach 65%. Improve form and posture. Focus on a solid front rack and bottom position. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Gymnastics

For time

3 rounds of

25 GHD sit-ups

25 toes to bar

 

Notes

Ideally go the GHD sit-ups unbroken and break the toes to bars into a big and a smaller set with short rest between. Alternatively, go the GHD unbroken for as long as possible and break the toes to bar into fast, smaller sets with minimum rest between. In any case it will be a challenge for your core and you might reach failure at some point. Make sure you don’t go all out from the start and don’t hesitate to break into really small, fast sets if needed.

Options

GHD sit-ups → weighted sit-ups (med-ball)

Toes to bar → knees to chest

Engine

A. For time

300 double-unders

100/75 calories, Assault bike

300 double-unders

 

Rest 10 minutes

 

B. For time

Row, 1500/1200 meters

300 double-unders

Row, 1500/1200 meters

 

Notes

Try to achieve maximum performance in each set. Go the double-unders unbroken for as long as possible, when you break take a breath and immediately continue with the remaining exercises. On the ergs maintain a steady, sustainable pace that will allow you to breath well. The difference between the bike and the row is that you should move fast on the bike yet partially recover, regulate your breathing and continue strong to the double-unders. In the case of the row you should pace the first meters, push through the double-unders as efficiently as possible and then maintain the fastest sustainable pace in the last meters.

Options

Assault bike → bike-erg → row
