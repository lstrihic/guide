Hang Power Snatch

2×3 @ 60%

1×2 @ 70%

1×2 @ 80%

2×1 @ 85%

3×1 @ 90%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 2-3 reps until your reach 60%. Improve explosiveness and hang position stability. Focus on pulling with proper angles (barbell near hips and vertically close to the body) and an explosive shoulder shrug. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Conditioning

AMRAP 20 Minutes of

20 single arm dumbbell hang clean & jerks 22,5/15kg – 50/35lb

20 double-unders

20 chest to bar pull-ups

20 double-unders

20 shoulders to overhead 52/37kg – 115/75lb

20 double-unders

20 front squats 52/37kg – 115/75lb

20 double-unders

 

Notes

Move steadily and constantly through the biggest part of the workout, 15+ minutes, then start pushing harder by increasing effort to go unbroken and shortening your transitions. Go the hang clean & jerks at a fast, steady tempo that will allow you to move efficiently. Go the double-unders fast and unbroken. Ideally go the chest to bar pull-ups and the front squats unbroken, alternatively break each exercise into a big and a smaller set with short rest between. If you have a good touch & go technique on the shoulders go the shoulders to overhead unbroken, if not make a plan to break into no more than 2-3 sets with short to minimum rest between.

Options

22,5/15kg – 50/35lb → 15/10kg – 35/25lb

Chest to bar pull-ups → pull-ups

52/37kg – 115/75lb → 43/30kg – 95/65lb

Deadlifts

5×2 @ 85%

 

Notes

Build up with sets of 3-4 reps < 60% and 2-3 reps < 85%. Improve strength. Focus on proper form (straight back, hips low, good use of legs) and solid lifts with full range of motion. Rest no less than 2 minutes between sets.

Strength/Gymnastics

For time

3 rounds of

21 ring dips

3 leg-less rope climbs

Then,

3 rounds of

15 bench press 75/45kg – 165/100lb

3 regular rope climbs

 

Notes

Maintain a fast tempo on the first couplet and push really hard the second one. Break the ring dips into no more than 2-3 set with minimum rest between. On the leg-less rope climbs move fast with proper and coordinated leg movement (kicks or kipping) and descent smoothly by sliding down and jumping. In the second couplet you will be under fatigue so make sure you break the bench presses into manageable sets but with minimum rest between. Most important be extra technical on the regular rope climbs, find solid footings and squat on the box in order to assist your upper body. The more technically you work on the rope the more efficiently you will push through the session.

Options

Ring dips → ring push-ups

Leg-less → regular rope climbs

75/45kg – 165/100lb → 55/35kg – 120/80lb
