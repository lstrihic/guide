Notes

Notes

Conditioning

For time

60 wall-ball shots 9/6kg – 20/14lb

30 bar muscle-ups

60 wall-ball shots 9/6kg – 20/14lb

Cap: 13 minutes

 

Notes

Ideally break the first wall-ball shots and the bar muscle-ups into a big and a smaller set with short to minimum rest between and go the last wall-ball shots unbroken for as long as possible breaking just 1-2 reps just before failure. Alternatively, break each exercise into no more than 3-4 manageable sets with short rest between. In general go smoothly until you complete all the muscle-ups and then push the wall-ball shots hard. Consider that your cardio will be challenged, make sure you breathe well and be technical on the pull-up bar.

Masters 40+

Strength/Gymnastics

AMRAP 15 Minutes of

30 toes to bar

30 overhead squats 43/30kg – 95/65lb

30 toes to bar

30 overhead squats 61/43kg – 135/95lb

30 toes to bar*

Max overhead squats 82/57kg – 180/125lb

 

Notes

The goal is to move efficiently until the Max-rep set and then be super consistent. Prefer to break the toes to bar into no more than 2-3 sets with minimum rest between. On the overhead squats break into as few sets as possible. As weight increases sets will be considerably smaller, yet make sure you keep a solid overhead position and move steadily for as long as proper form allows in each one. On the max-reps, less remaining time means more challenging sets, more remaining time means more sets but less challenging. Lift the bar from the ground each time.

*Note down the tie break.

Options

Engine

A. 3 sets of

Row, 1500 meters @ 22 strokes/minute

Rest 2 minutes between sets

Pace: 2km PR pace +16 seconds

Rest 5 minutes

 

B. 2 sets of

Row, 600 meters @ free rate

Rest 1 minute between sets

Pace: 2km PR pace + 7 seconds

 

Notes

Start the session by rowing 3 sets of 1500 meters. Keep steady 22 strokes/minute and try to achieve a 2km PR pace +16 seconds. Continue by 2 sets of 600 meters, select your strokes for the 600-meter distances and try to achieve a 2km PR pace + 7 seconds. Ideally, your strokes should be powerful and at a moderately fast rate, consider that fewer strokes mean more power and more strokes mean more cardiovascular taxing. Row 500 meters at very light pressure as cool down.
