Spilt Jerk

Daily Max

Then

3×1 @ 85%

 

Warm up

3 sets of

4 shoulder presses + 3 push presses + 2 split jerks @ 20/15kg barbell

Goal

Build up to the heavy lift for the day with sets of 3 reps < 75%, 2 reps < 85% and a few sets of single lifts > 85%. Don’t force a max weight lift, make sure you lift the heaviest weight comfortably. Once you find the daily max drop down to 85% and work on technique and explosiveness.

Movements

Focus on an explosive push, a deep and fast drive under the barbell and a solid reception. Keep your mind on your foot work and the stability of your shoulders.

Conditioning

AMRAP 11 Minutes of

7 overhead squats 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

10 meters, Handstand walk

14 chest to bar pull-ups

10 meters, Handstand walk

 

Warm up

Build up to 61/43kg – 135/95lb. of overhead squat

Then,

2 rounds of

12 shoulder taps

10 kipping swings pull-up bar

8 hand release push-ups

6 pull-ups

Goal & Movements

Move steadily through the biggest part of the session, 8+ minutes, and then start pushing harder by shortening your transitions. Ideally go each exercise unbroken with smooth transitions between them that will allow you to keep moving efficiently throughout the session. Alternatively break the overhead squats in a big and a smaller set with short to minimum rest between.

Options

61/43kg – 135/95lb. —> 52/37kg – 115/80lb. —> 43/30kg – 95/65lb.

Handstand walk —> 2 wall-walks

Chest to bar pull-ups —> pull-ups

*Go with pull-ups if you can’t perform at least 10 unbroken chest to bar pull-ups while fresh.

Deadlift

Daily Max

 

Warm up

3×6-8 deadlifts @ 20/15kg barbell

Goal

Build up to a comfortable heavy lift for the day with sets of 5 reps < 60%, 3 reps < 80% and a few sets of single lifts > 80%.

Movements

Focus on utilising your legs on each rep so as to take much effort away from your back.

Max Effort

For time

50/35 calories, Assault bike

30 toes to bar

Cap: 5 minutes

 

Warm up

3 sets of

9/7 calories, Assault bike @ fast pace

7 toes to bar

Rest 60 seconds between sets

Goal & Movements

Move fast and strong from the start. Start explosively on the bike and maintain a pace 72+/65+ RPM then go straight to the pull-up bar and go unbroken for as long as you can. Keep in mind that if you perform long kipping toes to bar you will be more consistent throughout but your reps will be slower. If you perform short kicking toes to bars then you will be taxed faster but your reps will be also faster.
