Squat Snatch

2×2 @ 60%

2×2 @ 65%

2×2 @ 70%

1×1 @ 75%

1×1 @ 80%

3×1 @ 85%

 

Warm up

Warm up with an empty barbell and increase weight progressively with sets of 2-3 reps until you reach 60%.

Goal

Improve explosiveness and technique. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

Movements

Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the barbell and a solid deep receiving position.

—

 

Snatch Pull

3×2 @ 90% of Max Squat Snatch

 

Warm up

Continue to the snatch pulls from where you left the squat snatches.

Goal

Work on pulling strength and technique (proper angles on the second pull). Go with single lifts, rest no less than 2 minutes between sets, no more than 5 seconds between lifts.

Movements

Focus on a slow, strong starting pull, once the barbell is over the knees increase your speed and finish with an explosive second pull to an almost standing position.

Conditioning

For time

33.27.21.15.9 reps of

Deadlifts 83/55kg – 180/120lb

Handstand push-ups

 

Goal

The descending reps give you the psychological advantage, make sure you keep pushing until the last second.

Movements

Ideally break both exercises of the high-rep rounds (33, 27 reps) into a big unbroken set and some smaller sets (5-10 reps) with short rest between and go the lower-rep rounds (21, 15, 9 reps) unbroken for as long as possible breaking each exercise 2-3 reps before reaching failure. Alternatively break all rounds into a big and some smaller manageable sets with short rest between that will allow you to keep moving efficiently.

Options

83/55kg – 180/120lb → 61/43kg – 135/95lb

Handstand push-ups → push-ups hand release

Strength/Gymnastics

For time

10 rounds of

4 double kettlebell snatches 24/16kg – 53/35lb

10 meters, Handstand walk

 

Goal

Focus on kettlebell mechanics and transitions.

Movements

Ideally go both exercises unbroken with smooth transitions between that will allow you to push through the total volume efficiently. Depending on your comfort on gymnastics or weights choose an exercise to go faster into. e.g if you are strong prefer to go straight from handstand walk to the kettlebells and smoothly from the kettlebells to the handstand walk, if you are good on gymnastics do the opposite.

Options

24/16kg – 53/35lb → 20/12kg – 44/26lb

10 → 7,5* → 5* meters of

Handstand walk → 2 wall-walks

*Choose a distance that you can easily perform unbroken while fresh
