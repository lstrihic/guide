Hang Squat Snatch

2×2 @ 65%

2×2 @ 70%

2×1 @ 75%

3×1 @ 80%

 

Warm up

3 sets of

3 snatch high pulls + 3 hang power snatches + 3 overhead squats 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 hang squat snatches  until 65%.

Goal

Improve technique and confidence at the hang position. The hang position is with the barbell 10cm above the knees. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Movements

Focus on a fast pull with an explosive shoulder shrug and a deep receiving position. Avoid spending too much time at the hang position.

Conditioning

A. For time

5 rounds of

15 toes to bar

7 overhead squats 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

Cap: 10 minutes

 

Rest 5 minutes

 

B. For time

5 rounds of

15 chest to bar pull-ups 9/6kg

5 squat snatches 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

Cap: 10 minutes

 

Warm up

2 sets of

10 toes to bar

5 overhead squats

10 chest 2 bar pull-ups

5 squat snatches

Rest 60 seconds between sets

*progressively increase weight on the barbell with each set until you reach the prescribed weight

 

Goal

Try to achieve maximum performance on each one of these two portions. The goal is to move fast and steadily through the 4 rounds with unbroken exercises and fast transitions between them and, if possible, push the last round hard.

Movements

Ideally go the toes to bar and the overhead squats unbroken with the fastest possible transitions that will allow you to do that. Alternatively break the toes to bar into a big and a smaller set with minimum rest between. In the 2nd portion, prefer to go the squat snatches with single lifts yet at a fast tempo. Push the last round hard in both portions.

Options

61/43kg – 135/95lb. —> 52/37kg – 115/80lb.

Strength/Gymnastics

For form

8.11.13 reps of

Double kettlebell snatches 24/16kg – 50/35lb.

Box-jumps over 76/61cm – 30/24”

Strict parallettes handstand push-ups 10/5cm – 4/2”

Then

20 meters, Double kettlebell front rack lunges 24/16kg – 50/35lb.

 

Warm up

2.4.6.8

Double kettlebell snatches < 24/16kg – 50/35lb.

Front rack lunges

Strict parallettes handstand push-ups

4 box-jumps over

Goal

Focus on proper form and kettlebell movement mechanics. Once you complete the triplet continue with the front rack lunges.

Movements

Ideally go each exercise unbroken, alternatively break the double kettlebell snatches and/or strict parallettes handstand push-ups into a big and a smaller set with short to minimum rest between. Maintain a fast sustainable pace on the box jump overs and break the front rack lunges into 2×10 meters unbroken ditances.

Options

24/16kg – 50/35lb. —> 20/12kg – 44/26lb.

Strict parallettes handstand push-ups —> regular strict handstand push-ups

Engine – Rowing

A. Row 3000 meters @ 2km PR pace + 16 seconds

 

Rest 3 minutes

 

B. Row 1500 meters @ 2km PR pace + 13 seconds

 

Rest 2 minutes

 

C. Row 750 meters @ 2km PR pace + 7 seconds

 

Rest 1 minute

 

D Row 375 meters @ Max effort

 

Notes

Strokes:

3000 meters @ 22/min

1500 meters @ 24/min

750 meters @ 28/min

375 meters @ free

Warm up

4 minutes @ easy pace using the warm up routines (1st minute only arm pulls, 2nd only leg pulls, 3rd and 4th minute regular rowing)

Rest 1 minute

2 sets of

2 minutes @ 2km PR pace + 16 seconds

Goal

The goal is to prime by progressively increasing your pace through 3 decreasing distance portions, and finish with a max effort distance of 375 meters. Row 3000 meters at 2km PR pace + 16 seconds, rest 3 minutes, row 1500 meters at 2km PR pace + 13 seconds, rest 2 minutes, row 750 meters at 2km PR pace + 7 seconds, rest 1 minute and finish with the max effort distance. Stroke rate is free, keep in mind that fewer strokes require more powerful pulls while more strokes cause more cardiovascular tax. Prefer to start with fewer strokes and, if needed, increase throughout the distances.

Cool down

4 minutes @ very light pressure.
