Warm up

3 sets of

4 snatch pulls + 3 hang power snatches @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 power snatches until 50%.

Goal

Improve confidence and stability. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Movements

Focus on a powerful pull and a steady receiving position. Keep the barbell close to your body’s vertical line. Hip crease should be over 90 degrees, shoulders tight and elbows locked out.

Snatch Pull

2×3 @ 95% of Max Power Snatch

2×2 @ 100%

2×1 @ 105%

 

Warm up

Continue to the snatch pull from the percentage that you stopped the power snatch.

Goal

Improve pulling strength.

Movements

Focus on a controlled starting pull until the barbell is just over the knees and then increase speed as you pull the barbell over hip height.

12 ring muscle-ups

Row, 800 meters

Cap: 15 minutes

 

Warm up

Row, 800 meters @ easy pace

Then,

2-3 sets of

4 kipping ring pull-ups

8 kipping  ring dips

10 thrusters @ 20/15kg barbell

Goal

Push steadily until you complete the thrusters and then start pushing harder for the strong finish.

Movements

Maintain a steady pace on the row throughout the session and increase to max effort as you reach the end of the last distance. On the bar muscle-ups ideally go unbroken, alternatively break into a big and no more than 1-2 smaller sets with minimum rest between. Prefer to push smoothly through the thrusters with manageable sets that will allow you to keep moving efficiently with short rest between sets.

Options

Ring muscle-ups —> chest to bar pull-ups

Strength Stamina

For form

30/23 calories, Assault bike

Then

5 rounds of

9 deadlifts 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

6 hang power cleans 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

3 shoulders to overhead 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

Then

30/23 calories, Assault bike

Then

5 rounds of

7 deadlifts 70/52kg – 155/115lb.

5 hang power cleans 70/52kg – 155/115lb.

3 shoulders to overhead 70/52kg – 155/115lb.

 

Warm up

Build up to 70/52kg – 155/115lb. of 3 deadlifts + 2 hang power cleans + 1 shoulder to overhead

Then,

2 sets of

12/9 calories, Assault bike @ fast pace

3 deadlifts + 2 hang power cleans + 1 shoulder to overhead 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

Rest 60 seconds between

Goal & Movements

The goal is to move steadily and efficiently with the barbell under fatigue. Move fast and explosively through the calories on the assault bike at a pace of 72+ for men / 64+ RPM for women. A good way to go the lifts of each round is by 8 deadlifts, 1 + 6 hang power cleans + 3 shoulders to overhead, then straight to the deadlifts with a short rest between each round taken between deadlifts and hang power cleans. Same for the 2nd part with the heavier weights. The more you manage to keep the 2nd part equal to the 1st part, the better your performance will be.
