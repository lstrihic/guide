Hang Power Snatch + Hang Squat Snatch

2×2+1 @ 60% of Power Snatch

1×2+1 @ 70%

1×1+2 @ 80%

5×1+2 @ 85%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 2 hang power snatches + 2 hang squat snatches until you reach 60%. Improve efficiency with the barbell on the hang position. Focus on explosive power snatches and technical squat snatches. The lifts at 60% are 2 times of 2 hang power snatches + 1 hang squat snatch, at 80% are 1 hang power snatch + 2 hang squat snatches. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Hang Power Clean + Jerk

2×3+1 @ 60% of Power Clean

2×2+1 @ 70%

2×1+2 @ 80%

5×1+2 @ 85%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 2 hang power cleans + 2 jerks until you reach 60%. Improve efficiency with the barbell on the hang position. Focus on explosive power cleans and technical jerks. The lifts at 60% are 2 times of 3 hang power cleans + 1 jerk, at 80% are 2 times of 1 hang power clean + 2 jerks. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Conditioning

For time

Run, 400 meters

6 rope climbs 4,6m – 15ft

21 double kettlebell front squats 24/16kg – 53/35lb

Run, 400 meters

5 rope climbs 4,6m – 15ft

15 double kettlebell shoulders to overhead 24/16kg – 53/35lb

Run, 400 meters

4 rope climbs 4,6m – 15ft

9 double kettlebell thrusters 24/16kg – 53/35lb

Cap: 21 minutes

 

Notes

The goal is to run at a fast steady pace throughout the workout and move efficiently on the rope. You should run fast because the distance is not long and running time will make a difference, yet make sure that your pace will not dramatically affect your performance on the rope and the kettlebells. Move fast and technically on the rope, find solid footings and squat to assist your arms, descend smoothly by sliding down (rope between feet) and jumping on the ground. Ideally go the kettlebell exercises unbroken, alternatively break into a big and a smaller set with short to minimum rest between. Perform fast transitions and try to regulate your breathing when running.

Options

24/16kg – 53/35lb —> 20/12kg – 45/26lb.

13 reps @ 65%

11 reps @ 70%

9 reps @ 75%

5 reps @ 85%

 

Notes

Focus on full range of motion and form. Ideally go each round unbroken for as long as proper form allows, when you feel you struggle, take a breath and continue. Alternatively break from the start into manageable sets that will allow you to lift efficiently. Take short yet adequate rest between rounds (+- 60 seconds).

Max Effort – Regionals 14.7

For time

64 pull-ups

8 overhead squats 93/61kg – 205/135lb

Cap: 6 minutes

 

Notes

Move explosively from the start, there is no time to waste. Go the pull-ups unbroken for as long as you can, break only 1-2 reps before you feel like reaching failure, take minimum rest and continue. The real challenge is the overhead squats, take a breath before you start your lifts. Ideally a heavy athlete could go for a snatch and continue with a solid touch and go set of overhead squats. Yet we recommend that you clean the barbell, place it on the back rack, jerk it to a solid overhead position and continue with the overhead squats. Don’t hesitate to push your limits, this is what training is all about.

Options

93/61kg – 205/135lb —> 83/54kg – 185/120lb.
