Snatch Balance + Overhead Squat

2×2 @ 60%

1×2 @ 65%

1×1 @ 70%

1×1 @ 75%

1×1 @ 80%

3×1 @ 85%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 2 snatch balances + 2 overhead squats until you reach 60%. The lifts at 60% are 2 sets of 2 reps of 1 snatch balance + 1 overhead squat, at 80% are 1 snatch balance + 1 overhead squat. Use racks or high blocks. Focus on an explosive drive under the barbell and a solid overhead position. Rest no less than 2 minutes between sets, no more than 15 seconds between reps.

Hang Power Snatch + Hang Squat Snatch

2×3 @ 60% of Max Power Snatch

1×3 @ 70%

1×2 @ 75%

1×2 @ 80%

5×1+2 @ 85%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and continue with sets of 2 hang power snatches + 2 hang squat snatches until you reach 60%. The lifts at 60% are 2 sets of 3 reps of 1 hang power snatch + 1 hang squat snatch, at 85% are 5 sets of 1 hang power snatch + 2 hang squat snatches. Improve hang position and work on explosiveness. Focus on a solid hang position (10cm above knees), an explosive pull and a fast drive under the bar (squat snatch). Rest no less than 2 minutes between sets, no more than 15 seconds between reps.

12 box-jumps over 60/50cm – 24/20”

9 power snatches 43/30kg – 95/65lb.

9 hang squat snatches 43/30kg – 95/65lb.

 

Rest 4 minutes

 

B. AMRAP 8 Minutes

12 box-jumps over 60/50cm – 24/20”

6 power snatches 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

12 toes to bar

6 hang squat snatches 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

 

Rest 4 minutes

 

C. AMRAP 8 Minutes

12 box-jumps over 60/50cm – 24/20”

3 power snatches 83/52kg – 180/115lb.

12 toes to bar

3 hang squat snatches 83/52kg – 180/115lb.

 

Notes

Try to achieve high performance in each part. The volume of the lifts decreases with each part and the load increases yet your effort should remain the same. Go the box-jumps at a steady, sustainable pace (ideally touch & go), prefer to go the power snatches with single lifts but make sure you keep minimum rest between reps. Ideally push the toes to bar unbroken, alternatively break from the start into sets (7-5, 6-6) that will allow you to keep moving efficiently. The challenge will be the hang squat snatches, ideally go unbroken for as long as possible, drop only 1-2 reps before failure. Focus on technique and a solid deep receiving position. Alternatively, make a plan to break from the start into a big and a smaller set with short to minimum rest between. The less you drop the bar, the better. Push harder as you reach the finish of each part attempting the unbroken and shortening the transitions.

Strength/Gymnastics

For form

30 strict handstand push-ups

30 deadlifts 102/70kg – 225/155lb.

30 kipping handstand push-ups

30 deadlifts 143/90kg – 315/200lb.

 

Notes

Focus on proper form and full range of motion. Make a plan to break each exercise into sets that will allow you to move efficiently through the total volume. The start with the handstand push-ups makes the whole workout a challenge. Prefer to break them into manageable sets (ideally no more than 6 in total) with short to minimum rest between, in any case make sure you don’t push too hard too early. In the same way break the deadlifts and kipping handstand push-ups (focus on fast reps with good kipping technique) into a big and some smaller manageable sets with short rest between. On the heavy deadlifts consider that sets will be smaller yet make sure you keep short rest between, don’t hesitate to go with single reps as you reach the end if needed.

Options

143/90kg – 315/200lb → 124/80kg – 275/175lb

Engine

5 sets of

Ski-erg, 500 meters @ easy to moderate pace

Ski-erg, 500 meters @ moderate pace

Ski-erg, 500 meters @ moderate to fast pace

Rest 3 minutes between sets

 

Notes

Work on your tempo. Each set is a 1500-meter distance. Start at an easy pace and progressively increase until you reach a challenging pace for the last meters of the set. Use the first set to find the progression that you can sustain and suits you best then try to achieve the same progression or higher in each next set.
