Power Clean with 2” pause below knees + jerk

2×2 @ 55%

1×2 @ 60%

1×1 @ 65%

1×1 @ 70%

1×1 @ 75%

2×1 @ 85%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and continue increasing load progressively with sets of 2-3 reps until you reach 55%. Focus on form and technique. Go for a solid steady position with the barbell below the knees, count 2 full seconds before you continue your pull. Prefer to practice power jerks. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between lifts.

Notes

Warm up with light loads with sets of 2 squat clean + 1 front squat + 2 jerks. The lifts at 60% are 2 times of 1 squat Clean + 1 front squat + 1 jerk, the lifts at 65% 1 squat Clean + 1 front squat + 1 jerk. Focus on a solid deep receiving position and explosive jerks. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Ski-erg, Calories

Deadlifts 102/70kg – 225/155lb.

Notes

The goal is to start steadily and push harder as cals/reps decrease. Pace the high-cal rounds of the ski and push harder on the low-cal rounds (be more explosive in each next round). On the deadlifts break the high-rep rounds into a big and some smaller sets with short to minimum rest between and go the low-rep rounds unbroken for as long as you can. Run at a fast yet sustainable pace between rounds that will allow you to regulate your breathing.

For form

10 snatches @ 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

10 snatches @ 75/52kg – 165/115lb.

10 snatches @ 83/57kg – 185/125lb.

 

Notes

Start with touch and go reps and switch to drop and go when you feel like you cannot sustain proper touch and go technique anymore or you get significantly slower. You can go with either power or squat snatches. But if you go with squat snatches prefer to push through with single lifts. Load the barbell on your own and make sure you practice fast weight increases.

Gymnastics

For form

10.20.30.40 reps of

GHD sit-ups

Meters, Handstand walk

5 ring muscle-ups

 

Notes

Focus on quality of movements and practice fast transitions. Go the GHD sit-ups unbroken for as long as possible at a steady pace, break the high rep rounds if you find that needed, but take minimum rest when you do that. On the handstand walk break all rounds into 10-meter unbroken distances, move fast with quick steps and a proper handstand position (toes pointing upwards, legs straight, tight arms and shoulders). Go the ring muscle-ups unbroken.
