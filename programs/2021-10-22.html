Squat Snatch

Daily Max

Then

3×1 @ 90%

 

Warm up

2 sets of

3 snatch high pulls + 3 hang power snatches + 3 overhead squats + 3 squat snatches @ 20/15kg barbell

Goal

Build up to a heavy lift for the day with sets of 3-4 reps < 70%, 2 reps < 80 and single lifts > 80%. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets. Once you find the daily max drop down to 90% and work on consistency on heavy lifts.

Movements

Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the barbell and a solid deep receiving position.

Snatch Pull

3×2 @ 90% of Max Squat Snatch

2×2 @ 95%

2×1 @ 100%

 

Warm up

Continue from the percentage you left the squat snatch.

Goal

Work on strength. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

Movements

Focus on a slow pull at proper angles and finish with an explosive shoulder shrug. Barbell should remain close to your body’s vertical line.

Conditioning

For time

300 double-unders

30 clean & jerks 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

30/23 calories, Assault bike

30 snatches 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

Cap: 20 minutes

 

Warm up

Build up to 61/43kg – 135/95lb. of 1 snatch + 1 clean & jerk.

Then,

2 rounds of

30 double-unders

3 clean & jerks

9/7 calories, Assault bike @ moderate pace

3 snatches

Goal & Movements

Go the double-unders unbroken for as long as you can, if you break take a short breathe and immediately continue with the remaining reps. Move explosively on the bike and push fast though the calories at a > 72 RPM for men | > 64 RPM for women. The challenge is to maintain a steady tempo on the lifts throughout the session. Prefer to go all the lifts with single reps at a tempo that will allow you to move constantly without any having to take at some point excessive rest between lifts.

Options

61/43kg – 135/95lb. —> 50/37kg – 115/80lb.

Warm up

1000 meters @ 22-24 strokes. 

Then,

3×250 meters @ Time Trial pace

Goal

Go for a 2000-meter official time trial. Go with free strokes and try to achieve maximum performance. This is an important session as it designates the reference point of the given paces that you work with in each session.
