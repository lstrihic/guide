Squat Clean & Jerk

2×3 @ 50%

2×2 @ 60%

1×2 @ 65%

1×1 @ 70%

1×1 @ 75%

1×1 @ 80%

1×1 @ 85%

2×1 @ 90%

 

Notes

Warm up with an empty barbell and progressively increase weight with sets of 2-3 reps until you reach 50%. Improve explosiveness and stability. Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the bar and a solid deep receiving position. Prefer to work on split jerks, go for a powerful push and a steady overhead reception. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between lifts.

Notes

Same as Monday. Continue to the clean pulls from where you left the squat clean jerks. Improve starting pull strength. Focus on a slow controlled pull with proper lifting angles. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

For time

8 deadlifts 102/70kg – 225/155lb

8 strict handstand push-ups

2 rope climbs

Notes

We follow the same workout format as on Monday. Start the first round at a fast manageable tempo and try to sustain it throughout the session. Ideally go all exercises unbroken with smooth transitions between, alternatively break the deadlifts and/or the strict handstand push-ups (prefer to break the one that you are not comfortable with) into a big and a smaller set with short to minimum rest between. Push the last round hard.

Engine

B. 3 sets of

Run, 1000 meters @ 3’30”-4’15”km

Rest 3 minutes between

 

C. 10 minutes @ very easy pace

 

Notes

The goal is to promote Vo2max and lactate threshold. Warm up running 15 minutes at an easy pace, and progressively increase your pace in order to prime cardiovascular function. Push your pace up to 90% of Max effort in each set. Recover by 3 minutes walking between sets and jog 10 minutes at a very easy pace as cool down.
