Squat Clean + Jerk

1×3+2 @ 60%

2×2+2 @ 65%

2×2+1 @ 70%

1×2+1 @ 80%

1×1+1 @ 85%

3×1+1 @ 90%

 

Warm up

2 sets of

3 clean pulls + 3 hang power cleans + 3 hang squat cleans + 2 presses + 2 push presses + 2 jerks @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 squat cleans + 2 jerks until 60%.

Goal

Improve explosiveness and stability. The lifts at 65% are 2 times of 2 squat cleans + 2 jerks. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 15 seconds between squat cleans.

Movements

Focus on an explosive pull, a fast drive under the barbell and solid receiving positions. Work and build confidence on split jerks since this is the most appropriate jerk technique when it comes to heavy weights.

Clean Pull

3×2 @ 90%

2×2 @ 95%

1×2 @ 100%

 

Warm up

Continue from the percentage that you left the power clean + jerk.

Goal

Improve strength. Go with single lifts, rest no less than 90 seconds between sets, no more than 10 seconds between lifts.

Movements

Focus on a controlled pull until the barbell is over the knees, then increase speed and finish with an explosive shoulder shrug.

Run, 200 meters

9 overhead squats 52/37kg – 115/80lb.

9 box jumps overs 61/50cm – 24/20”

Cap: 13 minutes

 

Warm up

Run, 400 meters

Then,

2 rounds of

7 overhead squats

7 box-jumps over

Goal

The goal is to run at a fast yet sustainable pace through the session that will allow you to perform well on the exercises and push harder as reps descend with each next round.

Movements

Ideally go the overhead squats unbroken, alternatively break the high-rep sets into a big and no more than 1-2 smaller sets with short to minimum rest between. On the box jump-overs try to go with touch and go technique maintaining a steady sustainable tempo in each round and make sure you push the last round really hard.

Options

52/37kg – 115/80lb. —> 43/30kg – 95/65lb.

C. For form

30 heavy double-unders

5 snatches 102/61kg – 225/135lb

30 heavy double-unders

5 clean & jerks 102/61kg – 225/135lb

 

Warm up

2 rounds of

10 heavy double-unders

3 snatches 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

10 heavy double-unders

3 clean & jerks 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

Goal & Movements

The goal is to move efficiently with the barbell under metabolic fatigue created by the double-unders. Try to achieve maximum performance in each set. Go the heavy double-unders unbroken, then ideally go the lightweight snatches and clean jerks with a big touch & go set and then switch to solid single lifts at a really fast tempo, alternatively start and finish with single lifts. On the heavier lifts prefer to start with single lifts yet focus on maintaining resting time between them minimum. Breathe well while you perform double-unders.

Options

A. 60% of Max Power Snatch

B. 75% of Max Power Snatch

C. 90% of Max Power Snatch
