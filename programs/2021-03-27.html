Conditioning

AMRAP 14 Minutes of

Row, 30/23 calories

20 overhead squats 43/30kg – 95/65lb

5 ring muscle-ups

 

Into

 

B2. 6 Minutes to find

1 Rep Max Snatch

 

Notes

This is a quarterfinals style workout. It is a total duration of 20 minutes. You have to perform well in both parts. Complete as many reps as you can of the triplet in 14 minutes. Move fast on the rower 1300+ for men / 1050+ for women and go the overhead squats and ring muscle-up unbroken. Perform smooth transitions that will allow you to do that. Warm up with a barbell before the workout reaching a load of about 80-85% of you 1rep max. Take about 2 minutes after the AMRAP to catch your breath, then go for 3 solid lifts starting from a load that was comfortably achieved during warm up.

Options

Ring muscle-ups → chest to bar pull-ups

Max Effort

For time

60 GHD sit-ups

5 legless rope climbs 4,7m

30 meters, Overhead lunges 61/43kg – 135/95lb

 

Notes

Move fast from the start, consider that the most difficult part is the overhead lunges and it is there where you should show confidence and consistency. Ideally go the GHD sit-ups unbroken for as long as possible. On the legless rope climbs ascend fast with proper and coordinated arm-leg movement and descend smoothly by sliding, extending your body and jumping on the ground. Break the overhead lunges into 10-meter unbroken distances, focus on a solid overhead position.

Options

Legless rope climbs → regular rope climbs 4,7m

61/43kg – 135/95lb → 43/30kg – 95/65lb

Engine

A. 2400 meters @ easy pace

 

B. 10 rounds of

100 meters @ 85-95% of Max effort

300 meters @ easy pace

 

Notes

Run 2400 meters (warm-up) and 1600 meters (cool down) at a comfortable pace of your choice. Perform 30-meter distances of front swings, side cross swings, front leg raises, toe taps… etc and finish with 3 sets of 50 meter sprints. Start from 85% of Max effort (100 meters) and increase to a pace near 93% of Max effort. Do not push each set to your limits, the aim is to focus on sprint mechanics and be consistent throughout. Jog for 300 meters in order to recover your breathing.

Options

10 → 8 → 6 rounds* of

100 meters @ 85-95% of Max effort

300 meters @ easy pace

*Stop if your 100-meter pace decrease more than 2 seconds
