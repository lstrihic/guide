Power Clean + Push Press

1×2+1 @ 60%

1×2+1 @ 70%

1×2+1 @ 75%

1×1+1 @ 80%

3×2+1 @ 85%

 

Warm up

3 sets of

3 clean pulls + 3 hang power cleans + 2 presses + 2 push presses @ 20/15kg barbell

Then,

Sets of 2 power cleans + 2 push presses until 60%.

Goal

The lifts at 60% are 2 power cleans + 1 push presses, at 85% are 3 times of 2 power cleans + 1 push press. Rest no less than 90 seconds between sets.

Movements

Focus on a powerful pull, a solid front rack receiving position and a strong push press. Make sure you utilise your legs to push the barbell overhead and create momentum before you continue with your shoulders.

—

 

Clean High Pull

2×5 @ 90% of Max Power Clean

2×3 @ 95%

2×2 @ 100%

 

Warm up

Continue to the clean high pull from the percentage that you stopped the power clean + push press.

Goal

Improve pulling strength.

Movements

Focus on a controlled starting pull until the barbell is just over the knees and then increase speed as you pull the barbell as high as possible. Do not force a pull with your arms, let the barbell gain height with the momentum created by the initial pull.

Conditioning – Amanda .35

For time

11.9.7.5.3 reps of

Ring muscle-ups

Squat snatches 61/43kg – 135/95lb.

Cap: 15 minutes

 

Warm up

Build up to 61/43kg – 135/95lb. of squat snatch

Then,

5 ring pull-ups

1 squat snatch

4 ring dips

1 squat snatch

3 ring muscle-ups

1 squat snatch

Goal

As reps in each round decrease use the psychological advantage and push harder, the more you are able to maintain a steady, solid tempo on the squat snatches the better your performance will be.

Movements

Ideally go the ring muscle-ups unbroken, the squat snatches of the high-rep rounds with single lifts at a steady tempo and if you feel confident enough finish the workout with a touch & go set of snatches in the round of 3 reps. Alternatively, break the ring muscle-ups of the high-rep rounds from the start into a big and a smaller set with short to minimum rest between and push harder as reps in each next round decrease.

Options

Ring muscle-ups —> Chest to bar pull-ups

61/43kg – 135/95lb.  —> 52/37kg – 115/80lb.

Max Effort

For time

21 bar-facing burpees over

15 deadlifts 143/90kg – 315/205lb.

9 bar-facing burpees over

Cap: 3 minutes

 

Warm up

Build up to 143/90kg – 315/205lb. of deadlift

Then,

2 rounds of

9 burpees

3 deadlifts

*1st round moderate pace, 2nd round fast pace

Goal & Movements

This is a sprint, go fast and push yourself to your limits. If you are confident with the heavy deadlifts push the bar-facing burpees over hard and go the deadlifts unbroken for as long as you can, if/when you break the deadlifts take minimum rest and continue. Don’t hesitate to switch to single lifts if you find that touch & go deadlifts start decreasing your performance. If you are not comfortable with the deadlifts, pace the first bar-facing burpees over so that you work as efficiently as possible on the deadlifts and go the deadlifts with either small touch & go sets or single reps from the start. Give everything you got on the last bar-facing burpees over.

Options

143/90kg – 315/205lb. —> 124/83kg – 275/185lb.
